DIY SCIENCE PROJECT

Microbial Fuel Cell

Materials: Two chambers (anode and cathode), salt bridge, organic waste material, bacterial culture, electrodes, and multimeter. Steps: Construct the microbial fuel cell with an anode chamber filled with organic waste and bacterial culture. Connect the anode and cathode with a salt bridge and measure the electrical output using a multimeter.

Solar Oven

Materials: Cardboard box, aluminum foil, plastic wrap, black construction paper, and tape. Steps: Line the inside of the box with aluminum foil. Place black construction paper at the bottom. Cover the top with plastic wrap to create an airtight seal. Place the box in direct sunlight and use it to cook simple foods like s'mores or hot dogs.

Pendulum Wave Experiment

Materials: Multiple pendulums of varying lengths, a frame to hang them, and a stopwatch. Steps: Construct pendulums with different lengths and attach them to a single frame. Simultaneously release the pendulums and observe the wave patterns that form. Analyze the periodic motion and resonance phenomena.
